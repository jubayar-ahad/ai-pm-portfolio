Jubayar should position as a senior digital product manager moving into AI-enabled product work, not as someone who has already owned production AI/ML systems. The resume supports a credible transition because his strongest evidence sits in the product foundations AI teams need: commerce workflows, checkout optimization, mobile apps, search and inventory discovery, fulfillment, payments, analytics dashboards, platform modernization, API integrations, stakeholder alignment, and SAFe delivery.

Core positioning statement:

> Senior Product Manager with 10+ years delivering B2B and B2C digital products across retail, eCommerce, mobile, checkout, fulfillment, enterprise workflows, and payments. Now focused on AI-enabled commerce, search, workflow automation, personalization, and decision-support products where strong discovery, data-driven prioritization, platform delivery, and risk-managed rollout matter.

Use the phrase "AI-enabled" more often than "AI product leader." It signals direction while avoiding overclaiming direct AI/ML ownership. The resume's proof is strong enough without inflating it: 67% fewer failed transactions, 45% faster page loads, 33% NPS increase, mobile growth to 68% of transaction volume, 40% app-transaction growth, 30% delivery improvement, 50% fraud reduction, and 20% retention improvement.

## Weekly Job-Search Routine

Use a repeatable weekly schedule so the transition does not become random application volume.

### Monday: Targeting and Applications

- Review 15-20 roles, save only those matching at least two evidence lanes: commerce, search, fulfillment, workflow automation, payments, analytics, platform modernization, or enterprise delivery.
- Submit 3-5 tailored applications.
- Customize the top third of the resume for each role.
- Track which resume proof points match the job description.

### Tuesday: Portfolio Build

- Spend 2-3 focused hours improving one artifact.
- Rotate between PRD, metrics plan, dashboard sketch, risk review, experiment plan, and interview narrative.
- Keep every artifact labeled as portfolio or transition work when AI is involved.

### Wednesday: Networking

- Send 5 targeted outreach messages to PMs, hiring managers, alumni, ex-colleagues, or recruiters.
- Lead with a specific lane: AI-enabled commerce, search, fulfillment, workflow automation, or payments/risk.
- Attach or mention one relevant portfolio artifact only when it strengthens the conversation.

### Thursday: Interview Practice

- Practice 2 behavioral stories and 1 AI PM case.
- Use the same structure every time: context, product problem, users, constraints, decision, tradeoff, result, and what would change in an AI-enabled version.
- Record weak answers and tighten them into 90-second and 3-minute versions.

### Friday: Market Feedback and Follow-Up

- Follow up on applications and networking conversations from the prior week.
- Review which roles got responses and which did not.
- Adjust headline, summary, keywords, or portfolio ordering based on actual market response.
- Add one learning to the job tracker.

### Weekend: Light Learning and Portfolio Polish

- Spend 60-90 minutes on AI PM learning: evaluation, RAG, semantic search, recommendation systems, responsible AI, or AI product analytics.
- Spend 60 minutes cleaning a portfolio artifact so it is recruiter-readable.
- Avoid endless courses without artifacts. The goal is to learn enough to prove product judgment.

## Application Filters

Prioritize roles that include:

- Commerce, checkout, search, fulfillment, marketplace, retail media, customer experience, workflow automation, internal tools, payments, risk, or analytics.
- PM ownership of discovery, requirements, roadmap, experimentation, metrics, launch readiness, and cross-functional delivery.
- AI as an applied product capability rather than a requirement to own model architecture or conduct data science research.

Be cautious with roles that require:

- Direct ML model training ownership.
- Deep data science or research background.
- Prior production LLM ownership as a hard requirement.
- Heavy infrastructure/platform AI engineering with little user-facing product work.
