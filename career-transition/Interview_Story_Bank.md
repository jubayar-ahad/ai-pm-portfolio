## 1. Discovery: Real-Time Inventory Search at Costco

**Use for questions like:**
- "Tell me about a product discovery process you led."
- "How would you validate an AI search or recommendation feature?"
- "How do you reduce risk before scaling a new product capability?"

**Situation:** At Costco, Jubayar was Product Owner for member-facing mobile app and web features. One discovery area was real-time in-store inventory search, where digital expectations had to align with physical warehouse realities.

**Task:** Validate whether the feature solved a real member need while accounting for operational constraints, data freshness, and rollout risk.

**Action:** He drove discovery and validation, partnered with UX and warehouse operations to map user journeys, used staged rollouts to manage risk, monitored task-completion metrics, and iterated before broader release.

**Result:** The resume does not give a quantified business outcome for this feature, so keep the result grounded: the work produced a validated, lower-risk rollout path and a measurement loop before broader release.

**AI PM answer angle:** "This is the same product muscle I would bring to AI search: define the user promise, validate whether the underlying data can support it, roll out in stages, monitor task success, and avoid making the experience feel more certain than the system really is."

**Do not say:** "I built an AI inventory search model." The truthful version is product discovery for inventory search, with a strong AI-adjacent transition angle.

- "My transition angle is AI-enabled commerce, search, fulfillment, automation, and decision support."

- "I owned ML personalization at Meijer."
- "I led AI fraud detection at North American Bancard."
